# Learnloom product brief

## Product summary

Learnloom is a personal learning service that turns topics a user cares about
into thoughtful, source-grounded daily lessons called Knowledge Dossiers.

A user gives Learnloom one or more topics they want to understand over time.
They can provide publications, websites, feeds, researchers, or other sources
they already trust. Learnloom continuously curates useful material from those
sources, connects it to the learner's previous Dossiers, and produces a new
structured lesson each day.

Learnloom will also be able to discover relevant sources across the web on the
user's behalf. This discovery capability is planned for launch but is not yet
implemented in the current product. Users will be able to rely on their own
trusted-source list, Learnloom's discovery, or a combination of both.

The result is not another feed and not a collection of AI summaries. It is a
growing, personal curriculum based on what is happening in the world now.

## The problem

The internet gives people unprecedented access to information, but access does
not automatically become understanding.

Curious people already know what they want to learn about. It might be climate
technology, artificial intelligence, behavioral science, urban planning,
markets, biotechnology, design, or several topics at once. The difficulty is
maintaining a high-quality learning practice around those interests.

Today, that requires the user to repeatedly do all of the following:

- Search for worthwhile new material.
- Decide which publications and authors are credible.
- Separate meaningful developments from noise, repetition, and hype.
- Read across several sources to understand the complete picture.
- Work out how a new idea relates to concepts they encountered previously.
- Turn explanations into mental models they can remember and apply.
- Organize what they learned somewhere they can find again.
- Repeat the entire process the next day.

Most people solve parts of this problem with bookmarks, RSS readers,
newsletters, read-later apps, note-taking tools, search engines, and general AI
assistants. These tools are useful, but the learner still has to assemble the
system and provide the discipline.

The common outcome is information abundance with very little continuity:

- Saved links accumulate faster than they can be read.
- Newsletters compete for attention in an already crowded inbox.
- Feeds optimize for recency and engagement, not comprehension.
- Search answers the question of the moment but does not build a learning path.
- Notes become disconnected fragments.
- AI summaries make content shorter, but often remove the mechanism, evidence,
  nuance, and productive difficulty that make learning durable.
- The same introductory ideas are encountered repeatedly because the system
  does not understand what the learner has already covered.

The real problem is therefore not “finding more content.” It is transforming a
continuous stream of changing information into a coherent body of knowledge.

## The central product idea

The user chooses the subjects. Learnloom takes responsibility for maintaining
the learning loop.

For each topic, Learnloom repeatedly:

1. Watches the user's trusted sources and, when enabled, discovers additional
   relevant material from across the web.
2. Filters out low-value, repetitive, weakly supported, or irrelevant items.
3. Selects a small set of sources that together create a useful learning
   opportunity.
4. Identifies the central question, mechanism, or development worth
   understanding that day.
5. Connects the new material to the learner's recent Learning History.
6. Produces a structured, cited Knowledge Dossier.
7. Adds that Dossier to a lasting personal learning archive.
8. Optionally delivers it by email as a gentle daily prompt.

Over time, each topic becomes a living curriculum. It remains current because
it draws from the changing web, but it remains coherent because new lessons
build on what the learner has already explored.

## What the user provides

A user can create multiple recurring learning streams. For each stream, they
define:

- A topic, question, or domain they want to understand.
- Their current level: beginner, intermediate, or advanced.
- The learning outcome they care about.
- The approximate lesson length.
- A daily schedule and time zone.
- Their preferred delivery and publishing settings.
- Trusted sources such as articles, publications, RSS or Atom feeds, research
  organizations, or expert websites.
- Whether Learnloom may discover additional sources beyond their supplied list.
- Whether clearly labeled AI Exploration is allowed.

Topics can be broad, such as “artificial intelligence,” or directed, such as
“how modern AI systems learn, fail, and affect real institutions.” The learning
goal helps Learnloom decide what deserves attention and how deeply to explain
it.

## Source modes

Learnloom is intended to support three ways of sourcing material:

### Trusted sources

The learner supplies the sources they already respect. Learnloom monitors and
curates from that bounded source set. This gives the learner maximum control
over the information environment.

### Learnloom discovery

Learnloom finds relevant material across the web based on the topic, learning
goal, learner level, source quality, recency, and the contents of previous
Dossiers. It should prefer primary sources, reputable research, strong
explanations, and diverse evidence instead of merely selecting popular pages.

This mode is planned and not yet implemented in the current application.

### Hybrid sourcing

The learner's trusted sources remain the foundation while Learnloom fills
important gaps with carefully selected outside material. For example, it might
combine a trusted publication with a primary research paper, public dataset, or
contrasting expert interpretation.

Regardless of mode, the origin of every Source Item should remain visible. A
learner should always be able to see what a Dossier was built from.

## What Learnloom creates

Each daily Knowledge Dossier is a compact lesson designed to produce
understanding rather than merely reduce reading time.

A Dossier can contain:

- The learning objective for the day.
- The central mechanism or mental model.
- Necessary prerequisites and context.
- A worked example that makes the idea concrete.
- Connections and synthesis across multiple sources.
- A skeptical review of assumptions, limitations, and competing explanations.
- A common misconception and why it fails.
- Retrieval questions that require the learner to recall the idea.
- A practical observation, exercise, or experiment.
- A continuity bridge to concepts from previous Dossiers.
- Citations and the original Source Items.
- Optional AI Exploration containing clearly marked analogies, deductions,
  scenarios, or experiments that are separate from sourced claims.

The goal is for a Dossier to feel like a thoughtful teacher prepared one focused
lesson from the best available material that day.

## Daily curation

“Daily” does not mean Learnloom must manufacture novelty every day. The product
should protect the learner's attention.

Depending on the topic and source activity, useful curation may mean:

- Explaining an important new development.
- Connecting several smaller developments into a larger pattern.
- Revisiting a difficult mechanism with a better example.
- Comparing two credible but conflicting interpretations.
- Filling a prerequisite gap revealed by previous lessons.
- Strengthening recall of an important older concept.
- Choosing not to amplify a story when the evidence is weak.

The value is editorial judgment plus instructional design, not simply a daily
generation schedule.

## Learning History and continuity

Every completed Dossier contributes a bounded record to Learning History:
concepts covered, source titles, learning objective, lesson summary, and recall
questions.

Learnloom uses this history to:

- Avoid teaching the same introductory explanation repeatedly.
- Refer back to concepts the learner has already encountered.
- Increase depth gradually.
- Identify missing prerequisites.
- Create thematic continuity between current events and foundational ideas.
- Revisit important concepts through spaced retrieval.
- Make each new lesson feel like the next step rather than an isolated article.

This is one of the most important differences between Learnloom and a generic
summarization tool. The unit of value is not one generated answer; it is the
growing learning journey.

## The personal learning home

Every learner can claim a personal Learnloom subdomain, such as
`maya.learnloom.blog`.

The Personal Site is the lasting home for their Dossiers. It provides:

- A chronological archive of completed learning.
- Organization by topic or learning stream.
- A permanent reading experience outside the inbox.
- A body of knowledge the learner can search and return to.
- Public or private visibility controlled by the learner.
- The ability to publish or hide individual streams and Dossiers.
- A shareable record of sustained curiosity when the learner wants one.

Email is a delivery channel, not the product's final destination. The Personal
Site turns daily lessons into something cumulative.

## Who Learnloom is for

Learnloom is for intellectually curious people who want to understand important
subjects over time but do not want to manually operate an entire research and
learning system.

Likely users include:

- Professionals keeping up with a rapidly changing field.
- Founders and operators learning across unfamiliar domains.
- Researchers following developments outside their core specialty.
- Students building depth beyond a fixed curriculum.
- Independent learners who already collect articles and notes.
- Writers and analysts maintaining awareness across several topics.
- Anyone who values understanding and recall more than feed consumption.

These users are not necessarily trying to complete a course. They want an
ongoing relationship with a subject.

## Jobs the product performs

For the learner, Learnloom should:

- Keep watch over subjects they care about.
- Find or monitor sources worth their attention.
- Decide what is worth learning today.
- Explain it at the right level.
- Preserve evidence and uncertainty.
- Connect it to what they already know.
- Help them remember it.
- Store it somewhere durable.

The desired emotional outcome is relief from information overload without
feeling disconnected from the world. The learner should feel informed,
oriented, and steadily more capable.

## Product principles

### Understanding over compression

The shortest possible answer is not the goal. Learnloom should preserve the
mechanism, evidence, examples, and questions needed for genuine understanding.

### Curation over accumulation

More links are not inherently valuable. Learnloom should select deliberately
and respect the learner's limited attention.

### Continuity over isolated answers

Every lesson should have a place in a longer learning journey.

### Sources remain visible

Source-grounded claims should remain traceable to their origins. Learnloom
should never make sourced material and model invention indistinguishable.

### Trust is user-controlled

Learners should be able to choose trusted sources, permit or restrict web
discovery, inspect what was used, and control what becomes public.

### Quality over forced frequency

A daily cadence should not justify low-value repetition or artificial novelty.

### A durable home over a disposable feed

The archive is a first-class product surface, not a by-product of delivery.

## How Learnloom differs from adjacent products

### Versus an RSS reader or news aggregator

An aggregator organizes incoming links. Learnloom selects across them and turns
them into a coherent lesson connected to previous learning.

### Versus a newsletter

A traditional newsletter reflects one publisher's editorial agenda. Learnloom
builds a personal publication around each learner's topics, goals, level, source
preferences, and history.

### Versus a read-later app

A read-later app stores the reading burden for later. Learnloom performs
curation and synthesis, then preserves the resulting understanding.

### Versus a generic AI summary

A summary is usually a one-off compression of one input. A Dossier synthesizes
multiple sources, teaches a mechanism, tests recall, exposes citations, and
continues an evolving curriculum.

### Versus an online course

A course is fixed in advance and eventually ends. Learnloom is responsive to
new developments and keeps growing with the subject and learner.

### Versus a search or chat assistant

Search and chat depend on the user knowing what to ask at that moment. Learnloom
maintains persistent interests and proactively creates the next worthwhile
lesson.

## Core product vocabulary

- **Knowledge Dossier / Dossier:** the finished, source-grounded learning
  artifact.
- **Newsletter:** a recurring learning stream containing a topic, sources,
  learner preferences, schedule, and publishing settings.
- **Source Item:** a normalized piece of reference material used for curation.
- **Issue:** one scheduled or manually requested occurrence of a Newsletter.
- **Learning Blueprint:** the instructional plan for one Dossier.
- **Learning History:** the bounded record of previous Dossiers used for
  continuity and reduced repetition.
- **AI Exploration:** an optional and explicitly synthetic section kept separate
  from source-grounded claims.
- **Personal Site:** the learner's public or private Learnloom subdomain and
  lasting archive.

## Current product boundary

The current application supports:

- Multiple topic-based learning streams.
- User-provided trusted web, RSS, and Atom sources.
- Learner level, goal, lesson length, and schedule configuration.
- A multi-stage Dossier pipeline.
- Source-grounded lessons, critique, practice, quality checks, and optional AI
  Exploration.
- Learning History for continuity.
- Manual and scheduled Issues.
- Optional email delivery.
- Public or private Personal Sites.
- Per-stream and per-Dossier publishing controls.

Automated discovery of sources across the broader web is a planned launch
feature and should be described as part of the intended product experience, but
not as functionality available in the current build.

## Product promise

Learnloom turns the subjects a person cares about into a living daily
curriculum—curated from the web, grounded in sources, connected across time, and
kept in a learning home that grows with them.

The core idea can be expressed simply:

**The web gives us endless things to read. Learnloom turns what matters into
something we can understand, remember, and build on.**
